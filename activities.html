Activities

Algonquin Park

Availability . . . . . . . Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter

Algonquin Park, the first provincial park in Ontario, protected from the noise and rush of civilization, this world-renowned park in Ontario is a sanctuary for the rugged beauty of the maple, pine, moose, and wolves. A Provincial Park since 1893, the 7500 square kilometers (3500 square miles) of Algonquin Park are home to a diverse and unblemished eco-system that can be found nowhere else on earth.

Swimming Pool, Patio, & Lake

Availability . . . . . . . Spring and Summer

To invigorate the body, the best wild swimming, a wonderful long swim off of our swimming dock in our clean lake is a must. Adjacent to the lake, the pool is part of a 5000 square foot, multi-level patio.

Kayaks & Canoes

Availability . . . . . . . Spring, Summer, and Fall

An important form of transportation for the Algonquin natives has always been the use of canoes on the beautiful lakes and rivers. Canoes and kayaks permit you to travel deep into Algonquin Park on our Galeairy Lake still as pristine as ever. Inquire at Sport Centre to go out super early next day (as early as sunrise)

Hiking & Biking

Availability . . . . . . . Spring, Summer, and Fall

The Couples Resort Hiking Trails - On-site scenic hiking trails, leading to a scenic view of Algonquin Park, consisting of three loops, Lookout Trail 1.0 km in length, Bear trail loop 2.7 and Rail Line loop 1.2

Motor, Paddle, & Row Boats

Availability . . . . . . . Spring, Summer, and Fall

Enjoy a free leisurely tour along the shores of Galeairy Lake and take in the beauty and subtle hues of Algonquin's spring colours. Or spend some time fishing for Trout, Bass, Pickerel. We have extensive collection of all weather clothing, fishing gear, outerwear, footwear, hats and accessories & apparel.

Wild Life Viewing

Availability . . . . . . . Fall

During late fall, you'll have a good chance of seeing a big bull Moose, its antlers now full-grown and polished, ready for the rut (mating season - give them lots of room). Fall evenings are the best time to try howling for wolves, you may want to try it yourself.

Feed the Canada Jay and Chickadee

Availability . . . . . . . Winter

Canada's New Official Bird; the Canada Jay and of course the Chick-a-dee! Touching Wildlife.

Famous birds that will eat seeds from your hand or your hat are the Canada Jay (aka the Gray Jay or the Whiskey Jack) and Boreal Chickadee.

Cross Country Skiing and Snow Shoeing

Availability . . . . . . . Winter

Tranquil with peacefulness. Algonquin Park has International quality ski trails; track set and skating lane. 48 kilometres of groomed and 23 kilometres of non-groomed cross-country ski trails available in three low-usage trail networks, typically December to early April. Check the latest ski trail conditions If you cannot go skiing you can always go IceHiking.
